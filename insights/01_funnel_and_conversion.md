# Funnel Overview (Product-Level Funnel)
Funnel:
View Item → Add to Cart → Begin Checkout → Purchase

Users:
- View Item: 13,427
- Add to Cart: 3,832
- Begin Checkout: 1,924
- Purchase: 1,069

Product Conversion Rate:
≈ 7.96%

Interpretation:
Roughly 8% of users who engage with a product eventually complete a purchase.
This isolates product page effectiveness and checkout performance, removing low-intent sessions.

Business Meaning:
Improvements to product pages and cart UX directly increase this metric and revenue.

# Funnel Dropoff Rate
~71% drop from view_item → add_to_cart
→ Product pages are the biggest bottleneck.

~50% drop from add_to_cart → begin_checkout
→ Cart friction, shipping surprises, or hesitation.

~44% drop from checkout → purchase
→ Checkout is relatively healthy compared to earlier stages.

# Time to Purchase
Summary:
- Median time to purchase: ~1 hour
- Average time to purchase: ~40 hours
- Fastest conversions happen within the same session
- Some users take up to ~26 days to purchase

Product Meaning:
The majority of buyers are impulse-driven, but there is a meaningful long-tail of considered buyers.

Product Actions:
- Optimize product pages for fast conversion
- Trigger cart abandonment reminders within 1–2 hours
- Run remarketing campaigns for at least 30 days

# Single vs Multi-Session Purchases
Results:
- 61.5% of purchases happen in a single session
- 38.5% require multiple sessions

Product Meaning:
The store is primarily impulse-driven, but a significant portion of revenue comes from users who need repeated exposure before purchasing.

Product Actions:
- Optimize product pages for fast decisions (images, pricing, shipping clarity)
- Trigger cart reminders within the same day
- Maintain remarketing campaigns for multi-session users

# Daily Conversion Trends
Results:
- Highest conversion rate: Friday (9.06%)
- Lowest conversion rate: Sunday (5.37%)
- Mid-week days outperform weekends

Product Meaning:
Purchasing behavior is strongest toward the end of the workweek, suggesting buying intent peaks on Fridays.

Product Actions:
- Schedule major promotions on Fridays
- Increase ad spend Thursday night → Friday
- Use weekends for awareness and browsing campaigns
- Push retargeting strongest on Friday
